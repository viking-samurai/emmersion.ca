Creative Collaborations

Learn to write fiction like a pro.

Let's face it, to many people, writing is hard. And writing serious fiction? Very hard. Between plot, character development, and creating settings that actually make sense, the task can be daunting. Add to that that it's often a required programme for most highschool diplomas, and the task can seem downright overwhelming. But fear no more, as E-mmersion.ca has a course designed specifically to help stuggling writers feel confident in abilities, and make it through their normal public education. While not everyone is destined to become a professional creative writer, at E-mmersion.ca, we can help you get through grade school, and restore some lost confidence along the way.

Why learn creative writing with E-mmersion?

Creative writing is an especially liberating art-form, as it allows a writer to bounce between the real and the fantastic without having to switch up their style or bounce between different works. There is an inherent assumption that creative writing is what it is, and that formal writing is what it is, but this is actually no more than that. An assumption. In reality, writing for an audience through a medium that is supposed to the relaxing to interpret actually forces the writer to concentrate on reality more than most people assume. By doing so, all the parts of the art-form actually come together much more readily, and create a more seemless and enjoyable experience for the audience.

Furthermore, creative writing is an excellent way to become a great writer in general. It's emphasis on story over facts allows writers a greater degree of flexibility in how they develop a story, which can be a lot less stressful than more formal syles of writing, such as an essay. For this reason, it can be an excellent tool for newcomers to Canada to imporve on the official languages of this nation. By not focusing so heavily on facts and proof, writers are more free to express themselves linguistically, and improve their sense of how either English or French looks and feelsinstead, as opposed to research-based writing, which gets boring and can become tiresome, causing people to learn the languages less efficiently.
